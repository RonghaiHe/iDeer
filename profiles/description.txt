I am a researcher focused on **Multi-Agent Systems, Graph Rigidity, and Sensor Network Localization**. My core research interests span the following areas:

1. Multi-Agent Formation Control - Cooperative control of networked robotic systems, distributed coordination, consensus, formation stabilization, formation maneuvering, leader–follower architectures, and scalable control strategies for large-scale multi-agent systems.

2. Graph Rigidity Theory - Distance rigidity, bearing rigidity, infinitesimal rigidity, persistence, and graph-theoretic methods for analyzing and guaranteeing formation shape maintenance, uniqueness, stability, and observability in networked systems.

3. Sensor Network Localization - Distributed and centralized localization algorithms, distance-only localization, anchor-free localization, relative pose estimation, and geometric optimization methods for recovering positions and orientations from network measurements.

4. Distributed Control and Estimation - Decentralized controller design, distributed optimization, cooperative state estimation, observer design, resilience to communication constraints, and robustness against measurement uncertainty in multi-agent networks.

5. Motion Planning and Trajectory Generation - Path planning, trajectory optimization, collision avoidance, formation reconfiguration, cooperative navigation, and motion coordination for autonomous robotic teams operating in dynamic environments.

6. Geometric and Optimization-Based Robotics - Geometric control, manifold-based estimation, constrained optimization, nonlinear system analysis, and the integration of estimation, planning, and control within a unified framework for autonomous systems.

I am NOT interested in: pure machine learning methods without geometric or control-theoretic foundations, computer vision tasks unrelated to localization or robotics, hardware design, network security, or general-purpose AI systems without applications to multi-agent coordination.
